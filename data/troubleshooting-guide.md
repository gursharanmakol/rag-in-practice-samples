# TechNova Troubleshooting Guide

This guide covers common issues with TechNova audio products. Follow the numbered steps in order for each issue. If the problem persists after completing all steps, contact TechNova Support at support@technova.com.

## Bluetooth Connection Issues

If your TechNova headphones will not connect or keep disconnecting from your device, follow these steps:

1. Open Settings → Bluetooth on your device.
2. Forget "WH-1000" from saved devices.
3. On the WH-1000, hold the power button for 7 seconds until the LED flashes blue.
4. Select "WH-1000" when it appears in your device's Bluetooth list.
5. Wait for "Connected" confirmation before playing audio.

If the headphones still disconnect intermittently, check that you are within 10 meters of the connected device with no major obstructions. Bluetooth performance degrades through walls, large metal objects, and near microwave ovens or other 2.4 GHz devices. If you experience disconnections only in specific locations, interference is the most likely cause.

For persistent connection issues after completing these steps, perform a factory reset (see the Factory Reset section below) and repeat the pairing process.

Note: The WH-1000 supports Bluetooth 5.2 and can maintain connections with up to two devices simultaneously through multipoint pairing. If you have multipoint enabled and are connected to two devices, audio playback from one device may be interrupted when the other device sends a notification or starts a call. You can disable multipoint pairing in the TechNova app under Settings → Bluetooth → Multipoint.

## Audio Quality Problems

If you hear distortion, crackling, or uneven audio balance, work through these steps:

1. Check that the 3.5mm cable (if using wired mode) is fully seated in both the headphones and your device.
2. Clean the 3.5mm jack and headphone port with a dry, lint-free cloth.
3. If using Bluetooth, ensure no other audio devices are connected to your source device that might be splitting the audio stream.
4. Test with a different audio source (another phone, laptop, or tablet) to determine whether the issue follows the headphones or stays with the original device.
5. Check the audio balance setting on your device — on most phones, this is under Settings → Accessibility → Audio Balance. Make sure the slider is centered.

If distortion occurs only at high volumes, this may be expected behavior at maximum output levels. Try reducing the volume by 10–15% and check whether the distortion disappears. If distortion occurs at moderate volumes, the driver may be damaged — contact TechNova Support for warranty evaluation.

## Battery and Charging Issues

If your headphones will not charge or the battery drains unusually fast, try the following:

1. Use the included TechNova USB-C cable. Third-party cables may not deliver adequate power for charging.
2. Connect to a power source that provides at least 5V/1A output. Low-power USB ports (such as those on older keyboards or monitors) may not charge the headphones reliably.
3. If the LED does not light up when plugged in, hold the power button for 15 seconds to perform a hard reset, then reconnect the charging cable.
4. Allow the headphones to charge for at least 30 minutes before attempting to turn them on if the battery was fully depleted.

Normal battery life for the WH-1000 is approximately 30 hours with ANC off and 20 hours with ANC on. If your battery life has dropped significantly below these numbers and the headphones are less than one year old, contact TechNova Support for a warranty battery evaluation.

The WH-500 has a rated battery life of approximately 8 hours. The smaller battery in the sport form factor is expected to provide shorter playback time than the WH-1000.

## Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) Issues

If ANC is not working or sounds unusual:

1. Make sure ANC is enabled. Press and hold the ANC button on the left ear cup for 2 seconds. You should hear a tone confirming ANC is active.
2. Check that the ear cushions are properly seated and forming a seal around your ears. ANC performance depends heavily on passive isolation — if the cushions are worn or not seated correctly, ANC will be less effective.
3. Clean the external microphones on each ear cup with a dry, soft brush. Debris or moisture on the microphones can cause ANC to produce unusual sounds or reduce effectiveness.
4. If ANC produces a hissing or rushing sound, try toggling ANC off and back on. If the sound persists, perform a factory reset.

ANC works best with consistent, low-frequency noise such as airplane engines, train noise, or air conditioning hum. It is less effective against sudden, sharp sounds like voices or keyboard clicks. This is normal behavior, not a defect.

The WH-1000 offers three ANC modes: High, Low, and Transparency. High provides maximum noise cancellation. Low reduces cancellation intensity for situations where you want some ambient awareness. Transparency mode uses the external microphones to pass through ambient sound, which is useful for conversations or hearing announcements without removing the headphones. Switch between modes by short-pressing the ANC button.

## Factory Reset

If other troubleshooting steps have not resolved your issue, a factory reset restores the headphones to their original settings. This will erase all saved Bluetooth pairings.

1. Turn off the headphones.
2. Press and hold the power button and the ANC button simultaneously for 10 seconds.
3. The LED will flash red and blue three times, then turn off.
4. Release both buttons. The headphones are now reset to factory settings.
5. Turn the headphones on and pair them again following the Bluetooth Connection steps above.

After a factory reset, you will need to re-pair the headphones with all your devices. Your EQ settings in the TechNova app will also be reset to defaults.

## Firmware Update Problems

If a firmware update fails or the headphones behave unexpectedly after an update:

1. Make sure the headphones have at least 50% battery before starting a firmware update.
2. Keep the headphones within 1 meter of your phone during the update process. Do not lock your phone or switch to another app while the update is in progress.
3. If an update fails mid-way, connect the headphones to power, turn them off, then turn them back on. The TechNova app should detect the incomplete update and offer to retry.
4. If the headphones are unresponsive after an update, perform a factory reset and then check the TechNova app for the latest firmware version.

For details on what each firmware version changes, see the Firmware Changelog.
